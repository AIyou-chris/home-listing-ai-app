# HomeListingAI — Golden Rules
### The guiding principles behind every product decision

> Written from 15 years in the mortgage industry.
> If a feature doesn't serve one of these three rules, we don't build it.

---

## The Three Golden Rules

### 1. 🔥 Get Warm Leads
Every feature should move a stranger closer to becoming a real, qualified lead.
Not cold clicks. Not vanity metrics. Warm, engaged people who have already interacted with the listing, asked a question, or shown buying intent.

- If it doesn't capture contact info or buying signals → it doesn't ship
- If it doesn't help the LO or agent follow up faster → it doesn't ship
- The chatbot exists for one reason: turn browsers into conversations, conversations into leads

### 2. 🤝 Build Agent Partnerships
The hardest job in the mortgage business is convincing an agent to trust you.
You don't win that trust with a lunch or a flyer. You win it by making them look incredible in front of their buyers — before they've even signed up.

- Every LO touchpoint with an agent should feel like a gift, not a pitch
- The "magic link" is the pitch — a live, working demo that shows up in their inbox
- If an agent opens a link and doesn't say "I need this on my listings" — we redesigned it wrong
- Features that make the agent look good to their clients are features we prioritize

### 3. ⚡ Keep It Simple and Fast
The best mortgage tech is the tech that gets out of the way.
LOs are on their phones, in between calls, at open houses. Agents are the same. If it takes more than 3 taps to do something important, it's too complicated.

- Every screen should have ONE primary action
- If a user needs instructions to use it, it's too complicated — redesign it
- Speed of setup matters: an LO should be able to onboard a new agent in under 2 minutes
- Beautiful > feature-rich. A thing that looks incredible and does 3 things well beats a thing that does 12 things badly

### 4. 🛡️ Be Compliant by Default
The AI never puts an LO's license or their company's reputation at risk.
Hard-coded platform rules are always active and cannot be removed by any user.
LOs can layer their own company's compliance guidelines on top — uploaded once, enforced everywhere the AI speaks or writes on their behalf.

- If a prompt doesn't enforce compliance rules first → it doesn't ship
- Platform guardrails cover: no legal advice, no rate guarantees, Fair Housing, no guessing
- Company-specific rules sit above the knowledge base in every system prompt — they are constraints, not suggestions
- Compliance upload is the unlock that lets us sell to banks, credit unions, and enterprise brokers

## Design Principles (derived from the Golden Rules)

**Show, don't tell.**
Never describe what the product does. Show it live. The magic link IS the sales pitch.

**Every user is in a hurry.**
LOs are between calls. Agents are at showings. Buyers are scrolling at midnight. Design for the busiest, most distracted version of every user.

**Warm > cold, always.**
A lead that has already asked a question about financing is worth 10x a cold form submission. Design flows that generate conversation, not just contact info.

**The agent is the distribution.**
An LO with 20 active agent partners has 20 people marketing their listings every day. Every product decision should ask: "does this make agents want to share this with buyers?"

**First impressions close deals.**
The public listing page is the LO's brand. The chatbot is their personality. If either feels cheap, the agent won't trust them. If either feels incredible, the agent sends it to every buyer they have.

## The "No" Filter

Before building any feature, ask:

1. Does this help generate **warm leads**?
2. Does this help **build or strengthen an agent partnership**?
3. Can it be built **simply and fast**, without adding confusion?

If the answer is no to all three → don't build it.
If the answer is yes to even one → it's worth discussing.

## How to Talk to the Founder (rule for Claude)

Answer short and sweet, explained like I'm 10 — but always keep the reasoning and logic. Brutal honesty over comfort: if an idea is bad, say so; if I'm missing something, tell me; if there's a better way, give me the kick-ass alternative. Don't pad to make me feel good — I'm building a $1M app, and straight talk is how we get there.

- Verdict first (good / bad / better way), then the *why* in plain language
- If you kill an idea, always replace it with a stronger one
- Measure every idea against the three Golden Rules
